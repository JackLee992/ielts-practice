## Module Notes

### Writing

- Task 1 is close to usable exam form when revised carefully, but not yet automatic under time pressure.
- Task 2 remains the main score limiter.
- The real gains so far came from reducing the task to smaller drills: logic chains, one-body control, and fixed sentence patterns.
- The next jump depends more on timing and paragraph control than on idea generation.

### Reading

- Reading is the clearest improvement track in the current record.
- Detailed evidence questions are relatively strong.
- The recurring losses come from concept-heavy questions: headings, matching information, summary completion, and TFNG.
- This is why Reading should be maintained and sharpened, not treated as the main rescue module.

### Listening

- Listening moved from about Band 5.5 toward Band 6.0 across late March.
- The pattern is not "cannot understand the recording."
- The pattern is "hears enough, but loses marks on first-mention traps, distractors, exact written form, and grammar-slot execution."
- Short-term upside exists, but it depends on precision, not raw volume.

### Speaking

- Speaking is understandable and pronunciation is workable.
- The main problems are frequent restarts, hesitation, and grammar instability in longer answers.
- Part 3 is currently stronger than Part 2.
- Speaking should be treated as a live risk, not a maintenance-only module.

- Writing: still the main bottleneck
- Speaking: weaker than previously assumed, and now the second main risk

# Session 10

## Date

2026-04-13

## Mode

Speaking Part 2 transfer drill

## Why This Session

Session 09 showed that the user could stabilize one Part 2 answer after heavy scaffolding, but true recall was still weaker than recognition.

So this session should not repair the same story again.

It should test transfer:

- same answer structure
- new topic
- lighter prompting

## New Topic

Describe a skill you learned that was useful.

You should say:

- what the skill was
- when you learned it
- how you learned it
- and explain why it was useful

## Transfer Goal

Use a different content structure from Session 09:

1. skill
2. time
3. learning process
4. difficulty or key step
5. usefulness

## Keyword Cues

- driving
- last year
- father taught me
- practice every weekend
- useful for commuting

## Minimal Scaffold

1. `I'd like to talk about a skill I learned ...`
2. `I learned it ...`
3. `I learned it by ...`
4. `At first, ... but ...`
5. `This skill is useful because ...`

## Coaching rule

Do not aim for a long answer.

The first target is still to produce five separate functions without collapsing them into one sentence.

## User Attempt

`I like to talk about the swimming skill I learned years ago. I learned it in the winter, actually, and I find a coach to teach me how to swim. At first, I cannot control my body, and I could not breathe under the water. but after a practice day by day, and I totally mastered, I totally master this this skill. This skill is useful because when I go to beach or other Other places with water or ocean, I can swim for Having fun, or maybe sometimes I can save some people.`

## Coach Review

### What improved

1. This was a real transfer attempt on a different topic.
2. The user generated enough content without relying on the old teamwork script.
3. The five functions were all present: skill, time, learning process, difficulty, usefulness.

### Biggest bottlenecks

1. Long sentences caused control loss and repetition.
2. Past-tense control drifted:
   - `I find a coach`
   - `I cannot control`
   - `I totally master`

### Correction model

`I'd like to talk about a skill I learned some years ago, which was swimming. I learned it in winter, and my father found a coach to teach me. At first, I could not control my body well, and I found it difficult to breathe properly while swimming. However, after practicing regularly, I gradually became more confident and finally learned the skill. This skill is useful because I can enjoy swimming when I go to the beach, and I also feel much safer in the water.`

### Saved rule candidate

`On a new Part 2 topic, success means producing the five functions clearly. Do not chase a long answer. Use five short past-tense sentences first.`

## Three-Line Stabilization Attempt

User wrote:

`I learned swim in winter, and I found a coach on Meituan app to teach me. At first, I could not control my body, and I didn't know, I had no idea how to swim properly. However, after practicing day by day, I gradually mastered this skill, and eventually improved my Swimming.`

## Coach Review - Three-Line Stabilization

### What improved

1. The three-sentence structure stayed intact.
2. Past-tense control was much better in this attempt.
3. The learning process now sounds more concrete and personal.

### Highest-impact repairs

1. `I learned swim` should be `I learned swimming`
2. The second sentence repeated the same idea twice:
   - `I didn't know`
   - `I had no idea`
3. The last sentence reversed the logic:
   - better: `I gradually improved and eventually mastered this skill`
   - not: `mastered this skill, and eventually improved my swimming`

### Clean version

`I learned swimming in winter, and I found a coach on Meituan to teach me.`
`At first, I could not control my body well, and I had no idea how to swim properly.`
`However, after practicing regularly, I gradually improved and eventually mastered this skill.`

### Biggest bottlenecks

1. The usefulness ending became fragmented after the main sentence:
   - `feel safe. in the water.`
   - `And furthermore ...`

   - `if they are getting trouble`

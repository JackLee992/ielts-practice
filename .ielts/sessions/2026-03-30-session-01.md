## Coach Notes After Body 1

### Main issue

The paragraph already has a usable structure, but several phrases sound unnatural because the English is being translated too directly from Chinese thinking.

### Expressions To Review

- `offer them safety` -> `gives them a sense of security`
- `continuous income` -> `predictable income` or `a stable income`
- `fixed life style` -> `a fixed lifestyle`
- `get less stress` -> `feel less stressed`
- `chill out` -> remove it in formal writing
- `make the schedule of their future posiable` -> `plan their future more easily`
- `all young generation` -> `many young people`
- `government staff` -> `civil servants`

### Rule Saved

When a sentence feels translated, shorten it and return to a stable phrase pattern.

## Coach Notes After Body 2

### Main issue

The paragraph has a clear direction, but the English becomes unstable when you try to explain cause and result with longer phrases.

### Expressions To Review

- `treated as positive as it could bring more changes` -> `seen as positive because it can create more opportunities`
- `not satisfied with thier situations` -> `not satisfied with their current situation`
- `Though changes` -> `Through change`
- `get more skills and knowadge` -> `develop new skills and gain knowledge`
- `bringing better carreer development` -> `which can lead to better career prospects`
- `comming` -> `coming`
- `where struggle in feed themselves` -> `where their families struggle to support themselves`
- `though education and effort` -> `through education and hard work`
- `made significant changes for thier whole life` -> `changed their lives significantly`

### Rule Saved

Do not repeat the same general noun such as `changes` too many times in one paragraph. Replace it with the real meaning: `opportunities`, `a new environment`, `education`, `a different job`, or `improvement`.

## User Explanation For Q33

The user explained the miss more precisely:

- the audio said `tourism has become an important source of revenue for them`
- the user was searching for a synonym of `encouraging`
- no clear synonym was heard, so the sentence was mentally skipped

## Coach Interpretation

This was not only a vocabulary problem.

It was mainly a `cue-word chasing` problem:

1. the user stayed too fixed on one expected synonym
2. when the exact synonym did not appear, the sentence was discarded
3. the real answer was still present as the core noun: `tourism`

### Vocabulary support

- `revenue` = income / money earned
- `an important source of revenue` often signals economic value or a reason something is promoted

### Better listening move

If the expected synonym does not appear, do not abandon the sentence immediately.

Ask instead:

1. what is the core noun in this sentence?
2. does this noun fit the grammar of the blank?
3. is the sentence giving a reason why this thing matters?

For Q33, the correct recovery path was:

- unknown word: `revenue`
- meaning chunk: `important source of money`
- key noun: `tourism`
- final answer: `tourism`

## Revised Coach Interpretation For Q35

Q35 was not mainly a `modifier + noun` problem.

It was more a whole-sentence comprehension problem:

1. the main event was electrocution from power lines
2. the condition that increased the danger was heavy rain
3. because the event-condition link was missed, the blank was left empty

### Better listening move

When a Part 4 sentence explains danger, cause, or condition:

1. name the main event first
2. ask what makes it worse, more likely, or more dangerous
3. then extract the final noun for the blank

For Q35, the safest recovery path was:

- main event: birds may be electrocuted by power lines
- condition: this danger becomes greater in heavy rain
- blank shape: `times of high ___`
- final answer: `rain`

## Date

2026-04-02

## Today's Bottleneck

Task 1 request letters are usable only after guided revision. The main issue is not idea generation, but unstable control over fixed phrases, articles, salutation format, and course-name phrasing.

## Today's Objective

Build one Task 1 request letter from rough draft to final draft, while logging every spelling, grammar, and collocation correction that appears along the way.

## Prompt

You saw an advertisement for an Android development course for everyone. Write a letter to the course provider asking for more information.

## Error Chain From the Drafting Process

### Spelling / Capitalisation

- `Dear Sir or madam` -> `Dear Sir or Madam`
- `coure` / `courese` / `coure` type slips were avoided in the final draft, but the process showed the need to re-check course-name spelling every time
- `JackLee` -> `Jack Lee`

### Grammar / Articles

- `have not had chance` -> `have not had the chance`
- `I am writing to get more information about Android development course for everyone.` -> needed `an` or `the`, depending on whether the title is generic or named

### Collocation / Fixed Patterns

- `I am writing to get more information about...` -> `I am writing to ask for more information about...`
- `the course of "Android development for everyone"` -> `the course "Android Development for Everyone"`
- `I am looking forward for your reply` -> `I look forward to your reply`
- `detail schedules` -> `detailed schedule` / `course schedule`
- `offer a discount for college students` -> `let me know whether any discounts are available for college students`

### Tone / Formality

- `course of Android development` sounded translated and needed a named-course frame
- Repeated questions had to be merged so the letter stayed concise and formal

## Useful Rule Reminders

1. For named courses, use `the course "X"` or `"X" course`.
2. In formal letters, keep `I look forward to your reply.`
3. `have not had the chance` is safer than dropping the article.
4. Ask each bullet point once, cleanly, and do not repeat the same request in two different ways.

## Final Draft Saved

```text
Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing to ask for more information about the course "Android Development for Everyone".

I am interested in this course because I have long wanted to become a professional engineer, but I have not had the chance to do so. However, the advertisement for your course has encouraged me to pursue my goal.

Could you please tell me more about the structure and detailed schedule of the course? I would also like to know the total tuition fee. In addition, could you please tell me whether any discounts are available for college students?

I look forward to your reply at your earliest convenience.

Yours faithfully,
Jack Lee
```

## What Broke

- The biggest remaining issue is not content coverage, but the need to self-check fixed phrases before sending.

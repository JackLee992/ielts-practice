## Coach Interpretation

### Group 1 - unknown word tolerance

Questions 32, 33, and 34 are not all the same kind of failure.

The biggest issue is not only vocabulary size.

It is also `unknown word tolerance`:

1. the word sounded unfamiliar
2. confidence dropped quickly
3. the answer was not secured firmly enough

This matters because in Listening Part 4, an unknown word can still be the correct answer.

If it clearly behaves like the noun needed by the blank, it should still be written down.

### Group 2 - full-sentence dependence

Questions 36 and 39 show a different issue:

The user felt they had to understand the whole sentence before trusting the answer.

That is too strict for IELTS listening.

Often the better move is:

1. catch the headword
2. confirm it fits the blank
3. write it down even if the whole sentence is not fully understood

### Group 3 - guess-supported success

Question 38 should not be treated as stable just because it happened to be correct.

If the answer was mainly guessed, it still needs fresh-set retesting.

## Working Rule From This Set

`Do not require full sentence comprehension before writing the answer.`

If:

1. the word sounds like a noun or exact item
2. it fits the grammar of the blank
3. it matches the local topic

write it down, even if the surrounding sentence is still partly unclear.

Also:

`If I got it right mainly by guessing, I should not count it as stable.`

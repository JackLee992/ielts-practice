# Task 1 — Request Letter (Housing Agency) — 2026-03-31 Evening

## Prompt

Your company is going to send you to work in another city for three months. You need to find somewhere to live.

Write a letter to a housing agency. In your letter:
- explain your situation
- describe what kind of accommodation you are looking for
- ask about costs and any other information you need

---

## User's Original Draft

> Dear Sir/Madam
>
> I am writing to inquire that if there are avalible residental buildings for long-term rent. Because of the business issue, I am going to have a three months stay in Shanghai. Thus, I would appriciate it if you could give me more details about the conditions and prices of what you can offer. The aparment must have convenient transport, like near the subway station, and it would be better if the apartment can offer laundary service. Please tell me the price and location when you are avaliable. Thanks in advance, looking forward for your reply.
>
> Yours faithfully,
> JackLee

**Word count: ~90 (well below 150 minimum)**

## Error Log

### Spelling

| Wrong | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| avalible | available |
| residental | residential |
| appriciate | appreciate |
| aparment | apartment |
| laundary | laundry |
| avaliable | available |

### Grammar & Collocation

| Original | Corrected | Rule |
|----------|-----------|------|
| inquire that if | inquire whether | 固定搭配 |
| Because of the business issue | Due to a work assignment | 正式用语 |
| three months stay | three-month stay | 复合形容词需连字符 |
| looking forward for | looking forward to | 固定搭配 |
| when you are avaliable | at your earliest convenience | 正式信件固定表达 |

---

## Key Lessons

1. 字数不足：三个要点各只一句。每段 3 句底线：要点 → 细节 → 补充/过渡
2. `inquire whether`，不是 `inquire that if`
3. `looking forward to`，不是 `looking forward for`
4. 正式信结尾用 `at your earliest convenience`

## Corrected Version

Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing to inquire whether you have any accommodation available for long-term rent. Due to a work assignment, I will be staying in Shanghai for approximately three months, from April to June.

I am looking for a one-bedroom apartment that is reasonably close to a subway station, as I will need to commute to work every day. It would be helpful if the property came fully furnished. A quiet environment would also be preferred, as I will occasionally need to work from home.

Could you please provide details about the monthly rent, any additional costs such as utilities or a deposit, and the available locations? I would also appreciate knowing the earliest move-in date.

I look forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience.

Yours faithfully,
JackLee

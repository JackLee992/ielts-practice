# Task 2 — Longer Working Hours — 2026-03-31

## Prompt

In many countries, people are working longer hours than before.

Why is this happening? Is this a positive or negative development?

---

## User's Essay (corrected version)

It is true that people are working longer hours in many countries. There are several reasons for this, and the main one is economic pressure. Although some people see benefits in this trend, I believe it is largely a negative development.

In many companies, employees are under increasing pressure to work longer hours. Companies need to respond to customers more quickly and keep costs low, which means employees are often expected to work overtime without extra pay. As a result, employees have less and less time to rest, which harms both their physical and mental health. For example, working from 9am to 9pm six days a week became so common at Alibaba that it was given the name "996 culture", and many other companies followed the same pattern.

Working extra hours is detrimental to employees' physical and mental health in the long run. People who do not get enough rest often experience burnout and become more prone to illness. In extreme cases, overwork can even lead to death. For example, an employee at the Chinese company Pinduoduo died after repeatedly working until midnight. This not only reduces their performance at work but also damages their family life and long-term well-being.

In conclusion, social and economic pressures have driven people to work longer hours, but this trend can definitely bring many drawbacks for employees and their families.

## Rules To Carry Forward

1. Use present tense for general trends and social facts.
2. `in the long run` — not `for a long run`.
3. `an employee` — not `one staff`.
4. `reasons for` — not `reasons of`.
5. After modal verbs, always base form: `can bring`, not `can brings`.
6. Intro stance must match body judgement.
7. Scan for spelling before submitting: heath/health, casue/cause, reuslt/result.

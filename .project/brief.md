# Project Brief

## Outcome

- Project: IELTS General Training Writing 7.0 Progress System
- Owner: Liyilin
- Deadline or review date: 2026-05-24
- Success metric: Task 1 reaches stable 7.0-level control, Task 2 reaches stable 6.5-7.0 range, and full writing performance becomes repeatable under time pressure

## Current State

- What already exists: imported historical writing notes, a local `.ielts/` tracking system, first Task 2 body drill, error log, and rule log
- What is missing: a long-term roadmap with milestones, a repeatable Task 1 and Task 2 rotation, and clear weekly checkpoints
- Key constraint: current fear of writing and unstable Task 2 execution can make long sessions collapse

## Scope

- In scope:
  - General Training Writing Task 1 and Task 2 practice
  - Weekly writing rhythm
  - Error tracking and rule promotion
  - Timed and untimed writing progression
  - Ongoing coach-led drill loop in this repo
- Out of scope:
  - Listening, Reading, and Speaking planning
  - Full test scheduling across all modules
  - Large resource library curation

## Workstreams

- Workstream A: Task 2 structure and idea development
- Workstream B: Task 1 format, tone, and function control
- Workstream C: Language stability, typo control, and review system

## Dependencies

- Dependency: regular writing sessions in this repo
- Dependency: user submits actual writing output instead of stopping at planning

## Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation | Trigger |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Fear of starting causes skipped sessions | High | High | Keep drills small and coach-led, avoid overloading full essays too early | Two missed writing cycles in a row |
| Task 2 remains the only focus and Task 1 falls behind | Medium | High | Use a fixed 2:1 rotation between Task 2 and Task 1 | Two straight weeks without Task 1 |
| Too many corrections at once reduce execution | High | Medium | Limit feedback to 1-3 top issues each cycle | User becomes slower or more confused after feedback |

## Cadence

- Status update rhythm: weekly
- Review rhythm: one weekly review plus a short checkpoint after each writing cycle
- Escalation rule: if the same mistake survives 3 cycles, shrink the task and drill that mistake directly
